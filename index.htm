Mentoring

At UNC I've had the opportunity to be a graduate student mentor for the Directed Reading Program (DRP):

The Directed Reading Program (DRP) pairs undergraduate students with graduate student mentors for semester-long independent study projects. It is an opportunity for motivated students to get one-on-one mentorship as they learn about a math topic of their interest but is out of the scope of the courses offered at UNC. Topics can include specific problems of interest, more in-depth learning about a course topic, or an introduction to higher level math.
  • Fall 2022: I mentored Marleigh Purgar-Mcdonald on the basics of algebraic geometry and commutative algebra. We followed roughly the treatment given in Ideals, Varieties, and Algorithms by Cox, Little, and O'Shea.
  • Spring 2023: I mentored Aryan Kokkanti on the basics of topology and knot theory. We followed some of the contents Colin Adams' The Knot Book.
  • Fall 2023: I mentored Paul Hamrick on algebraic geometry by going through Miles Reid's Undergraduate Algebraic Geometry. Our main goal for the semseter was to understand the proof of 27 lines.
  • Spring 2024: I mentored Yan Zhu on number theory and basic abstract algebra. We followed roughly some of Ireland and Rosen as well as relevant parts of Dummit and Foote.
  • Spring 2025: I mentored Yuxin Ge on the basics of field and Galois theory from some sections of Milne's notes. In particular, we focused on understanding fields and field automorphisms.
  • Fall 2025: I will be mentoring Vanessa Almelor this semester. We plan on going through Ideals, Varieties, and Algorithms by Cox, Little, and O'Shea.
